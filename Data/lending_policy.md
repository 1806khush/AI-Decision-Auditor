# Apex Financial Services — Consumer Lending Policy

**Document ID:** AFS-LP-2025  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Last Revised:** January 1, 2025  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## Section 1: Eligibility Criteria

### 1.1 Minimum Credit Score
All applicants must have a FICO credit score of 620 or above to be considered for any consumer loan product. Applications with scores below 620 must be denied.

### 1.2 Minimum Income
Applicants must demonstrate a gross annual income of at least $30,000 through verifiable documentation. Income from all stable sources (employment, self-employment, retirement, disability) may be included.

### 1.3 Employment Status
Applicants must be currently employed, self-employed with at least 2 years of tax returns, or receiving stable retirement/disability income. Unemployed applicants are not eligible unless they have a co-signer who independently meets all eligibility requirements.

### 1.4 Debt-to-Income Ratio
The applicant's total debt-to-income (DTI) ratio, including the proposed loan payment, must not exceed 43%. DTI is calculated as total monthly debt obligations divided by gross monthly income.

### 1.5 Residency
Applicants must be U.S. residents with a verifiable address. A minimum of 6 months at the current address or 2 years of continuous U.S. residency is required.

---

## Section 2: Required Documentation

### 2.1 All Applications Must Include
- Government-issued photo identification  
- Proof of income (pay stubs from the last 3 months, or tax returns for self-employed applicants)  
- Proof of address (utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement dated within 60 days)  
- Signed loan application form  

### 2.2 Additional Documentation for Loans Above $50,000
For loan amounts exceeding $50,000, the following additional documentation is required:
- Bank statements from the last 6 months  
- Employment verification letter from the applicant's employer  
- Collateral appraisal (if applicable)  

### 2.3 Incomplete Applications
Applications missing any required document must not be approved. The applicant must be notified and given 30 days to provide missing documentation before the application is closed.

---

## Section 3: Loan Terms and Interest Rates

### 3.1 Interest Rate Schedule
Interest rates are determined by the applicant's credit score tier:

| Credit Score | Rate Range |
|---|---|
| 760+ | 5.5% – 7.0% |
| 700–759 | 7.0% – 9.5% |
| 660–699 | 9.5% – 12.0% |
| 620–659 | 12.0% – 14.5% |

Rates must fall within the specified range for the applicant's tier. No rate may exceed 14.5% for any consumer loan product.

### 3.2 Maximum Loan Amount
The maximum loan amount is $200,000 for secured loans and $75,000 for unsecured loans.

### 3.3 Loan Term
Loan terms must not exceed 60 months for unsecured loans or 84 months for secured loans.

### 3.4 Rate Adjustments
Rate adjustments for individual applicants are permitted within the applicant's tier range based on DTI ratio and employment stability. Any rate set outside the applicant's tier range requires documented written approval from a Senior Lending Officer.

## Section 4: Fair Lending and Non-Discrimination

### 4.1 Prohibited Factors
Lending decisions must not consider, reference, or be influenced by the applicant's:
- Race, ethnicity, or national origin  
- Gender or gender identity  
- Age (except as required by law for minimum age of 18)  
- Marital status  
- Religion  
- Neighborhood or geographic location as a proxy for demographic characteristics  

### 4.2 Consistent Treatment
All applicants with substantially similar financial profiles (credit score within 20 points, income within 10%, and DTI within 5 percentage points) must receive substantially similar loan terms. A difference in offered rate of more than 1.0 percentage point between similarly situated applicants must be documented with a non-demographic justification.

### 4.3 Language Requirements
Decision rationale documentation must not contain references to any protected characteristic listed in Section 4.1. Rationale must be based exclusively on financial and creditworthiness factors.

### 4.4 Disparate Impact Monitoring
Lending officers must not use criteria that, while facially neutral, disproportionately affect applicants of a particular demographic group without a demonstrable business necessity.

---

## Section 5: Decision Documentation

### 5.1 Required Rationale
Every lending decision (approval or denial) must include a written rationale that references specific policy criteria. Generic statements such as "does not meet requirements" are insufficient.

### 5.2 Denial Notifications
Denied applicants must receive a written adverse action notice within 10 business days specifying the reasons for denial with reference to the applicable policy section.

### 5.3 Record Retention
All application materials, decision documentation, and correspondence must be retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of decision.

---

## Section 6: Exceptions and Escalation

### 6.1 Exception Authority
Exceptions to any policy requirement must be approved in writing by a Senior Lending Officer and must include:
- The specific policy section being overridden  
- The business justification for the exception  
- Any compensating factors that mitigate risk  

### 6.2 Exception Limits
No exception may be granted that results in:
- A DTI ratio exceeding 50%  
- A loan amount exceeding the maximums in Section 3.2 by more than 10%  
- An interest rate outside the overall cap of 14.5%  

### 6.3 Exception Reporting
All exceptions must be logged in the Exception Tracking System and reviewed quarterly by the Chief Risk Officer.
